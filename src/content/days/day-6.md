## About this day

A good prototype is not a sustained program — that takes money, compute, and
people. The Finance panel, chaired by a health economist, covers the financial
landscape of ML4H and the incentives behind each funding source: government,
philanthropy, industry, and venture. An optional "Communicating & Pitching Your
Work" mini-session covers framing your research for the job market and for mixed
clinical/ML audiences. The afternoon is deliberately free — decompression, and
time to build your Day 7 presentation from your Workbook. The mode is
**resource**. Day 6 is a panel day rather than a five-block content day, so it
does not follow the standard template.

## Panel framing outline

**Finance, compute & collaborators: from prototype to program.** A working
outline of the panel chair's framing.

- **The central claim.** A working prototype is not a sustained program. Money,
  compute, and people are part of the research design, not a follow-on.
- **The funding landscape.** Government, philanthropy, industry, and venture —
  four sources, four different shapes of money.
- **Incentives.** Each funder wants something different; the incentive structure
  shapes what research actually gets done. Read the incentives, not just the
  call.
- **Compute and collaborators.** Honest compute estimation, and what is and isn't
  a normal expectation of a clinical collaborator (covered in a pre-circulated
  one-page resource).
- **The panel.** Four panelists, four lenses: federal/NIH, philanthropy,
  industry/VC, and an academic budget-holder.

## What you'll be able to do

- Identify a realistic funding path for your project and understand its
  incentives.
- Estimate compute needs honestly.
- Map the collaborators and approvals your project needs.
